Life Expectancy Analysis

This project investigates the factors influencing global life expectancy across different countries from 2000 to 2015. Data is sourced from the World Health Organization (WHO) and the United Nations.

  • 📝 Analysis Report & Findings
  • 📊 Interactive Dashboard

Scope & Hypothesis

Our objective was to identify the primary driving forces behind life expectancy. We tested whether economic prosperity is the main driver, or if basic public health measures (like immunization) and societal infrastructure (like schooling) weigh more heavily.

1. The Economic Divide

We compared the overall distribution of life expectancy against GDP per capita to see if economic prosperity translates directly to longer lifespans.

Code 
# Scatter plot for GDP per capita vs Life Expectancy
fig_curr = px.scatter(
    df, x="GDP per capita", y="Life Expectancy", 
    title="Life Expectancy vs GDP Per Capita",
    opacity=0.6,
    color="Region",
    hover_data=['Country']
)
fig_curr.show()

Finding: Developed countries consistently show considerably longer life expectancies. However, GDP per capita, while positively correlated, has diminishing returns. Once a baseline GDP is met, health and societal metrics take over.

2. Impact of Education

Next, we analyzed how education (measured in years of schooling) impacts life expectancy.

Code 
# Scatter plot for Schooling vs Life Expectancy
fig_edu = px.scatter(
    df, x="Schooling", y="Life Expectancy", 
    title="Influence of Schooling on Life Expectancy",
    opacity=0.6,
    color_discrete_sequence=['#2CA02C']
)
# Add an OLS trendline if statsmodels is available, else just the scatter
fig_edu.show()

Finding: Interestingly, Schooling presents one of the strongest positive correlations with life expectancy among all metrics. Education generally correlates highly with better health consciousness, occupational safety, and higher income levels.

3. Mortality & Immunization

Finally, we looked at how basic health indicators like Adult Mortality affect life expectancy.

Code 
# Scatter plot for Adult Mortality vs Life Expectancy
fig_mort = px.scatter(
    df, x="Adult Mortality", y="Life Expectancy", 
    title="Adult Mortality vs Life Expectancy",
    opacity=0.5,
    color_discrete_sequence=['#D62728']
)
fig_mort.show()

Finding: Adult Mortality and Under 5 Deaths show an extremely strong negative correlation with life expectancy. Basic healthcare factors (including immunization for Polio and Hepatitis B) form a crucial baseline for standard medicine that drastically improves overall population lifespans.

import pandas as pd

import plotly.express as px

# Load the dataset

# Create an interactive scatter plot
fig = px.scatter(
    df, 
    x="GDP per capita", 
    y="Life Expectancy", 
    hover_data=['Country', 'Year'],
    color='Economy Status - Developed',
    opacity=0.6,
    title="Life Expectancy vs GDP per capita"

fig.show()
