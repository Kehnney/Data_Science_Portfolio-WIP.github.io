Life Expectancy Analysis

This project looks at which factors are most closely associated with life expectancy across countries from 2000 to 2015. The notebook focuses on schooling, GDP per capita, mortality, immunization, region-level averages, and a few comparison checks between developed and developing countries.

Dataset And Cleaning

The dataset covers 193 countries from 2000 to 2015. The two economy-status columns were combined into a single “Developed” or “Developing” label to keep comparisons easy to read.

Schooling And Improvement Over Time

The notebook also asked whether more schooling was linked to faster improvement in life expectancy from 2000 to 2015.

Code 
def improvement_rate(group):
    group = group.sort_values("Year")
    if len(group) >= 16 and group["Life Expectancy"].iloc[0] > 0:
        start = group["Life Expectancy"].iloc[0]
        end = group["Life Expectancy"].iloc[-1]
        return (end - start) / start * 100
    return np.nan

developing = df[df["Status"] == "Developing"].copy()
developing_rates = developing.groupby("Country").apply(improvement_rate).reset_index(name="Improvement Rate")
developing_2000 = developing[developing["Year"] == 2000][["Country", "Schooling"]].merge(developing_rates, on="Country", how="left")

world_rates = df.groupby("Country").apply(improvement_rate).reset_index(name="Improvement Rate World")
world_2000 = df[df["Year"] == 2000][["Country", "Schooling"]].merge(world_rates, on="Country", how="left")

schooling_growth = pd.DataFrame({
    "Scope": ["Developing countries", "World"],
    "Correlation with Schooling": [
        developing_2000["Schooling"].corr(developing_2000["Improvement Rate"]),
        world_2000["Schooling"].corr(world_2000["Improvement Rate World"]),
    ]
})

Conclusion

The analysis points to three consistent patterns:

  • Education is the single strongest positive factor. Countries with more years of schooling tend to have longer lifespans and improved faster from 2000 to 2015.
  • Child and adult mortality are the strongest warning signs. High death rates in young children and working-age adults are closely tied to lower life expectancy across every region.
  • Wealth matters, but it is not the whole story. Wealthier countries tend to live longer, but once basic income needs are met, health infrastructure, vaccination coverage, and education explain more of the difference.
